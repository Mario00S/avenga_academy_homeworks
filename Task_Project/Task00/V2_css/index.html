My Favourite Hobby

Hi I am Mario and my favourite hobby is ski touring.
I have started the journey in ski touring 3 years ago, and never looked back.
Ski touring is also known as ski mountaineering, which is a great oportunity for someone to be active and experience the joy of skiing, or hiking and then ski down the slopes.

How have I discovered this sport?

I was lucky enough that my dad was experimenting with this, couple of years before getting a second pair of skis, so that's why I got the oportunity to try out for myself. Before I started ski touring, I had not skied for about 15 years, so that was an issue to some extent. Learning first to ski is the main prerequisite. Additionally good mountaineering skills are very important.

Why it's so interesting?

When going on a tour especially on a sunny day, you get yourself into the lonely mountains accompanied by a small group of people that like to spend the day in the nature as well. As Macedonia is not a good ski destination, ski touring is a great way to skip to long ques while waiting for the ski lifts and get away from the crowd. The other thing is that during the winter the mountains are not visited by a lots of people and especially if you go on parts of the country that do not have a ski centers you are in for a treat.

How to get started?

Coming from my personal experience, the best thing to get into ski touring is to start mountaineering, you need to be prepared to do tours that typically cover 1000 meters of elevation, and about 5-10 km in one way. Hiking during the winter is another discipline as you need to prepare yourself with a lot of equipement, not just buying the equpement but learning how to use it. At first more advisable thing would be to do tours on-piste with more experienced ski touring mentors.

Important things about ski touring

Here is a list that is an overview of what things are important and need to be taken into consideration.

  1. Uphill Travel Skills
    • Efficient step-ski-slide movement
      1. Maintain proper posture for balance and efficiency
      2. Use short, deliberate strides on steeper slopes; longer strides on gentle terrain
      3. Glide skis without lifting, maximizing traction and conserving energy
    • Proper use of climbing skins
    • Correct ski pole technique
  2. Safety and Emergency Preparedness
    • Avalanche knowledge and equipment (transceiver, probe, shovel)
    • Layering and proper clothing for thermal regulation
    • Emergency skills and first aid knowledge
  3. Equipment and Transitions
    • Owning quality, well-fitting boots
    • Understanding how to transition between uphill and downhill modes
    • Carrying all necessary gear
